The Monster Bed

Willis J/Varley S

Beaver

A humorous tale in rhyme of a monster who’s scared to go to sleep in case there are any humans hiding under his bed! Watch for the twist in the tail when a little boy unexpectedly arrives lost in the wood, having run away from school!

The Castle of Fear

Burston Patrick

Walker Books

An activity book where the child engages in a personal journey with choices. Good to stimulate fantasies and discussion

There’s a Sea in my Bedroom

Wild Margaret

Picture Puffins

David is frightened of the sea; until one day he enters a friendly sea world in his imagination through holding a shell to his ear – and he finds that its fun!

The Ghost Eye Tree

Martin Junior Bill/Archambault John

Picture Puffins

About bravery in the face of real fear. Helpful when used with care and thought – could evoke anxiety. Also touches on ridicule and support. Very evocative pictures – and story too!

Jessica and the Wolf

Lobby Ted

Imagination press

‘Helping children develop and draw upon their internal resources to overcome fears, fantasies and bad dreams, ….. Jessica succeeds in vanquishing the wolf in her dream.’

Benjamin’s Dreadful Dream

Baker Alan

Picture Lions

About Benjamin the hamster’s fearful dream. Better used with adult support.

The Tunnel

Browne Anthony

Red Fox Picture Book

Courage in the face of fear. Includes unexpectedly supportive sister.

Mog in the Dark

Kerr Judith

Picture Lions Collins

Mog the cat is left outside in the dark imagining sorts of things out there away from his family. He is quite relieved when they come to take him in at last. A helpful introduction to our own need for someone to care for us, and for a safe place to be too.

I’m coming to get you

Ross Tony

Picture Puffins

Feared monster turns out to be little - and not scary after all.

Suddenly

McNaughton Colin

Picture Lions

A book to stimulate discussion of what might happen next – and turn over the page!

Who’s Afraid of the Big, Bad, Wolf.

Bradman Tony/Chamberlain Margaret

Methuen - Lift the flap book

A lift the flap picture book of the famous rhyme, - turning fear into a game we can play with a friend or a ‘grown- up’ beside us, to help us bear it and ‘come out the other side’.
