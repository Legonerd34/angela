The Castle of Fear

An activity book where the child engages in a personal journey with choices. Good to stimulate fantasies and discussion

The Ghost Eye Tree

About bravery in the face of real fear. Helpful when used with care and thought – could evoke anxiety. Also touches on ridicule and support. Very evocative pictures – and story too!

Lobby Ted

About Benjamin the hamster’s fearful dream. Better used with adult support.

The Tunnel

Red Fox Picture Book

Courage in the face of fear. Includes unexpectedly supportive sister.

Mog in the Dark

Kerr Judith

Picture Lions Collins

Mog the cat is left outside in the dark imagining sorts of things out there away from his family. He is quite relieved when they come to take him in at last. A helpful introduction to our own need for someone to care for us, and for a safe place to be too.

I’m coming to get you

McNaughton Colin

Who’s Afraid of the Big, Bad, Wolf.

Bradman Tony/Chamberlain Margaret

Methuen - Lift the flap book

A lift the flap picture book of the famous rhyme, - turning fear into a game we can play with a friend or a ‘grown- up’ beside us, to help us bear it and ‘come out the other side’.

Nightmares, Poems to trouble your sleep.

Prelutsky John

Adam and Charles Black

Gory poems with gory pictures! Children can feel safe and protected and even strong when they survive their ‘favourite’ nightmare poem being read to them in a safe trusted atmosphere.
